Ferrari Enzo

Specifications

Production Years 2002 - 2004
Engine 6.0L V12
Power 660 HP
Transmission 6-speed manual
Features Lightweight construction, advanced aerodynamics, iconic design

Overview

The Ferrari Enzo is a tribute to the legendary founder, Enzo Ferrari. It encapsulates the spirit of Ferrari with its groundbreaking performance and striking design, making it one of the most coveted hypercars in automotive history.

History & Development

Launched in the early 2000s, the Enzo was developed as a technological showcase by Ferrari. It brought together cutting-edge engineering and design, drawing inspiration from Formula 1 technology to create an unmatched driving experience.

Chassis & Aerodynamics

Constructed with a state-of-the-art carbon fiber monocoque, the Enzo’s design emphasized minimal weight and maximum rigidity. Its aerodynamic profile ensures exceptional stability and downforce at high speeds.

Engine & Performance

Powered by a naturally aspirated 6.0L V12 engine that delivers 660 horsepower, the Enzo offers thrilling acceleration and a driving experience that epitomizes Ferrari’s commitment to performance.

Manufacturing & Assembly

Hand-built in Maranello, Italy, each Ferrari Enzo is a masterpiece of engineering and craftsmanship. Its limited production run ensures exclusivity and a lasting legacy.

Mileage & Efficiency

While the Enzo is primarily designed for high performance, its engineering allows for surprisingly competitive efficiency figures given its raw power and capabilities.

Safety & Driver Assistance

Equipped with robust braking systems, a reinforced chassis, and driver-focused controls, the Enzo provides a secure driving experience without compromising on the exhilarating power it delivers.

Additional Information

The Enzo's cockpit is designed with a minimalist approach—focused on performance and driver engagement. Advanced telemetry and premium materials complete this iconic hypercar’s design.
