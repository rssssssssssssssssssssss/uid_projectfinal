Ferrari LaFerrari

Specifications

Production Years 2013 - 2018
Engine 6.3L V12 Hybrid
Power 950 HP
Transmission 7-speed dual-clutch automatic
Features HY-KERS hybrid system, carbon fiber monocoque, active aerodynamics

Overview

The Ferrari LaFerrari is a groundbreaking hypercar that merges F1-inspired hybrid technology with Ferrari’s legendary V12 engine, offering unmatched performance and innovation.

History & Development

Unveiled in 2013, the LaFerrari was Ferrari’s first hybrid hypercar, developed as a successor to the iconic Enzo. It represents a fusion of advanced aerodynamics and electrified performance.

Chassis & Aerodynamics

The LaFerrari features a carbon-fiber monocoque chassis designed for extreme lightweight performance and rigidity. Active aerodynamics optimize stability and efficiency at high speeds.

Engine & Hybrid Powertrain

Combining a naturally aspirated 6.3L V12 with an electric motor, the LaFerrari generates a total of 950 horsepower. The hybrid HY-KERS system boosts acceleration while enhancing efficiency.

Manufacturing & Assembly

Hand-built in Maranello, Italy, the LaFerrari was produced in limited numbers, ensuring exclusivity. Only 499 coupes and 210 open-top Aperta versions were made.

Mileage & Efficiency

Despite its immense power, the hybrid system provides improved fuel efficiency compared to traditional hypercars, offering a balance of speed and sustainability.

Safety & Driver Assistance

Equipped with advanced stability control, carbon-ceramic brakes, and active aerodynamics, the LaFerrari ensures both high-speed stability and driver safety.

Additional Information

The LaFerrari’s cockpit is a blend of high-tech displays and luxury materials, designed for maximum driver engagement and comfort in a race-inspired environment.
